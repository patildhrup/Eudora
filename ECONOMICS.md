# Economics

## Lead value estimate

- Target value per qualified lead: $250
- Expected conversion to paid pilot: 6%
- Expected revenue per pilot: $4,200 ARR

## CAC assumptions

- Zero-budget organic acquisition: $0 to $20 per lead
- Paid early-stage ads / sponsorships: $50 to $120 per lead
- Realistic first-stage blended CAC: $80 per lead

## Conversion funnel math

| Stage | Conversion | Example volume |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Website visitors | baseline | 1,000 visitors |
| Audit starts | 12% | 120 starts |
| Audit completes | 40% | 48 completed audits |
| Qualified leads | 25% | 12 leads |
| Paid pilots | 6% | 3 pilots |

- 1,000 visitors -> 48 completed audits
- 48 audits -> 12 qualified leads
- 12 leads -> 3 pilots

## Revenue projections

- Pilot MRR: 3 pilots × $350 = $1,050
- Annualized: $12,600 ARR from first 1,000 visitors
- If 10 qualified leads convert at 6%: 10 × $4,200 = $42,000 ARR

## What needs to happen to reach $1M ARR

- Convert 240 pilots to paid customers at $4,200 ARR, or
- Grow to 4,000 qualified leads with a 6% conversion rate, or
- Increase average revenue per pilot to $8,000 ARR and convert 125 pilots.

## Realistic assumptions

- Early traction is slow: the first 100 audits are mostly discovery.
- Pricing accuracy is the primary product moat; if it is wrong, conversion falls sharply.
- Step-function growth requires strong referral or community distribution.
- A single high-value lead can justify early development runway.
